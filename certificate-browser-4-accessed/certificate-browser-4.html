Hey there, fellow netizens, let's dive into a topic that has been causing quite a buzz in the world of internet security: CAPTCHR. It's the new and improved captcha that not only tests if you're human or not but assigns you a "human certificate". Sounds good, right? Wrong.

The idea of a "human certificate" might seem harmless at first, but it's actually quite concerning. Think about it, who decides who is "human enough" to access certain websites? A private company, that's who. This is eerily similar to how fascist governments and other regimes have used identity documents to control and discriminate against certain groups of people.

The concept of a "human certificate" raises questions about the fairness of access to information on the internet. It creates a system of privilege and exclusion, where those who are deemed "human enough" get to access certain websites, while others are locked out. This goes against the very principles of a free and open internet, which should be accessible to everyone regardless of their background.

CAPTCHA was pretty frustrating for most but particularly people with disabilities such as visual impairment. CAPTCHR is even worse! Now we have a situation in which not only is it impossible for certain disabled people to access websites, they now get denied a “human certificate” - whatever that is? By introducing a human certificate, the evil company behind CAPTCHR has deemed people unable to complete their CAPTCHRs unhuman.

On TOP of that, the use of a "human certificate" will open the door to discrimination against other groups of people, based on factors such as race, gender, or socio-economic status. This company can choose arbitrary criteria for determining who is "human enough" and can be biassed towards any certain group of people - who's holding them to account??

As users of the internet, we must be vigilant and speak out against any attempts to use digital certificates as a means of control and exclusion. Instead, we should advocate for a more equitable and democratic internet where everyone has equal access to information and resources.

Ultimately, CAPTCHR might seem like a harmless upgrade to the traditional captcha to keep up with whatever synthetic media chucks out, but the concept of a "human certificate" is deeply concerning. It raises questions about fairness, access, and discrimination. Let's stand up for our right to a free and open internet, where everyone is treated with dignity and respect.

F*!k CAPTCHR,
